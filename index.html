The largest galaxies

and curiosities about them.

IC 1101

v IC 1101 is the largest galaxy in the universe. The galaxy is elliptical to lenticular and appears with a golden-yellow hue. The brightest galaxy is located over 1.04 billion light-years away from Earth.

Phoenix Cluster

The Phoenix Cluster is one of the most robust galaxy clusters in the universe. It is located approximately 5.7 billion light-years away from Earth. The elliptical galaxy is composed of average and dark intra-group matter.

NGC 262

The NGC 262 is located 202 million light-years from Earth and is one of the largest known spiral galaxies. The galaxy has a diameter of approximately 1.3 million light-years.

NGC 4889

The NGC 4889 is also known as "Coma B," a supergiant elliptical galaxy situated approximately 308 million light-years from Earth. It was discovered by the British astronomer Frederick William Herschel I in 1785.

NGC 4874

The NGC 4874, also known as "Coma A," was discovered in 1785 by the British astronomer Frederick William Herschel I. It is located 350 million light-years away from Earth. It is a smooth, ball-shaped galaxy surrounded by a stellar circle.

A2261-BCG

The A2261-BCG is located approximately 3 billion light-years from Earth. It has an elliptical shape and was discovered by the Hubble Space Telescope. The A2261-BCG has a diameter of one million light-years.
